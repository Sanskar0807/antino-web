Why has Facebook Launched the CatchUp App even after having WhatsApp and Messenger for Calling?

02 June, 2020

Social media giant Facebook already had a Messenger and WhatsApp calling app. And now it has come up with the new calling application called CatchUp, an audio-only calling app. What is its importance in today’s time?

CatchUp is an audio-only app that can let people make group calls with 8 people.

Facebook accounts are not required for using CatchUp. It can access your phone’s contact list of users after taking permission.

It shows the availability of your friends and family which makes it easier to connect with them. It also allows a group of 8 people to connect together.

The app let users to set their status as “ready to talk” or “offline”.

CatchUp is not introduced officially as it is being tested in the U.S. and soon it will move to the next step for iOS users. This application is experimental and is still subject to change.

“Keeping in touch with friends and family is important, especially during this time of physical distancing. Messaging and video calling are great ways to send a quick update or connect with someone face-to-face, but speaking to someone over the phone offers a unique balance of both convenience and personal connection”.

It is proficient in coordinating phone calls and in setting group calls for upto 8 people that makes it easier for friends and family in the U.S. to coordinate voice calls.

CatchUp, the new audio-only calling app is basically Messenger Rooms for the voice calls.

It provides the capacity to see when your friends are online and ready to receive your call, as it shows who is ready to chat now.

You can join in-progress group calls as well. Catching up has never been this easy.

This app is developed by the New Product Experimentation team of the company with the idea of coordinating call timings with family members and friends. Users can check the availability of those whom they want to talk to.

Feel free to call your friends and family more frequently now. According to the ‘New York Times’, phone call volume has increased more as compared to internet usage during the pandemic. This new app will enable your friends and family members to see if you are available to take calls or not in the “Ready to Talk” section.

Studies found that people don’t call friends and family more frequently as they don’t know when they are available to talk. They are afraid of reaching them at an inconvenient time. Facebook’s NPE Team developers have tried to solve this problem by developing this app. CatchUp calling app addresses this problem and makes group voice calls extremely easy on one tap.

With the capacity of connecting 8 people together in a voice call by checking their availability makes it much better and convenient specially for those who feel hesitation in calling someone because they fear of calling them at the wrong time.

It will help people in designating a common time for calls and the best part is, users don’t need a facebook account to use it. This new platform will work with the contact list of your phone. However, facebook has mentioned in a blog that NPE apps are experimental and subject to change.
