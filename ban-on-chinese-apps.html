Ban on Chinese Apps

02 June, 2020

The Indo- Chinese border heated up in the middle of 2020 and the ripples have been felt even in the India IT space . The Indian government has blocked a host of Chinese mobile apps on the grounds that they have been engaging in activities threatening national security and public order.

These included popular social media apps like TikTok and social media apps like PUBG which had a huge Indian market. While the politics of this decision are complicated, the consequences of this decision effects the Indian technological and IT space .

There is no denying that India is a big market for IT services . India's young demography, internet penetration and wide usage of smartphones is attractive for IT service providers worldwide.

While the Indian government seeks to use its vast market for Internet services as a leverage to counter China, the Chinese app ban opens up new opportunities for the Indian app alternatives to capture the market space available as a consequence of banning of Chinese apps.

There are a few risks with this approach, especially given Indiaâ€™s global ambitions in technology.

There is always a risk of generating a tit for tat response between the two countries in the larger realm of technology and business space. China, being an important player in the technology global supply chain, will be hard, if not impossible, to ignore.

Also such a protectionist approach discourages global investors and Internet companies. While Chinese companies and technology have faced global backlash due to lack of transparency globally, India needs a to present itself as a rules-based market that promotes innovation and competition .

We at Antino Labs believe in the idea of promoting Make in INDIA. We believe in transparency and fair competition that allows true innovation to flourish.

We are most concerned about the ongoing debate on data security and privacy of our customer's data.

Within our organisation we have introduced data secure protocols and are eager for the implementation of data protection law by the government.

Apart from this safeguard , Government regulation must not hamper innovation and enable open competition . We are always eager to adapt to the rising competition and craft the best mobile apps and IT services .
